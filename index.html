Nature

Research shows that time outdoors in nature greatly benefits childrens’ learning and development, is critical for physical health, and promotes active, engaged, meaningful learning.

Play

Research on the effects of play continue to show positive benefits: play inspires creativity, builds confidence, and develops cognitive, critical thinking, and motor skills.

Outdoor Learning
The Benefits of Outdoor Learning
Children learn about and understand the world through their outdoor classroom experiences.

Physical

An increase in physical development, capability, and activity, which set up patterns for an active, healthy lifestyle.

Cognitive

Stronger problem-solving and communication skills. Developing an interest in science and math through connecting with nature.

Psychological

Higher, more positive self-esteem. Relationship building in a cooperative environment. Social-emotional mastery.

World View

Familiarity with and appreciation of nature and stewardship for the environment. Wide, expansive view of how the world works.
